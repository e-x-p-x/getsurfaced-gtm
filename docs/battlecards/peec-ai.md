## Why Surfaced Wins

### A | Actionable Content Generation (Not Just Analytics)
Peec AI's most cited limitation is that it shows you the data but doesn't tell you what to do about it. Surfaced runs gap analysis and then generates optimised content — going from insight to action in one platform.

### B | Content Quality Workflow
Surfaced includes prompt versioning, human-in-the-loop review, and automated quality scoring. Peec AI is a monitoring dashboard with no content creation or quality control capabilities.

### C | Lower Entry Price with More Features
Surfaced Starter at €49/mo includes gap analysis + content generation + quality scoring. Peec AI Starter at €85/mo gives you monitoring dashboards only — no content tools, no gap analysis.

## Their Weaknesses

**Limited Actionability:**
- The platform tells you what is happening but offers very little guidance on what to do about it. Users need separate tools to act on insights. ([Rankability](https://www.rankability.com/blog/peec-ai-review/), [Marketer Milk](https://www.marketermilk.com/blog/peec-ai-review))
- No content generation, no gap analysis, no optimisation workflows.

**Technical & Enterprise Gaps:**
- No public API (gated behind expensive Enterprise plan). Prevents integration with other tools and automation. ([Rankability](https://www.rankability.com/blog/peec-ai-review/))
- Lacks SOC-2 compliance and SSO — poor fit for security-conscious organisations. ([Rankability](https://www.rankability.com/blog/peec-ai-review/))

**UX & Reporting Issues:**
- Dashboard feels like a "data rabbit hole" — confusing visualizations with similar colors make it hard to extract quick insights. ([Growverge](https://growverge.com/peec-ai-review-2025-trial-marketers/))
- No brand sentiment tracking despite positioning as an analytics platform. ([Marketer Milk](https://www.marketermilk.com/blog/peec-ai-review))

**Pricing Transparency:**
- No free trial — prospects must pay before they can evaluate the tool. ([Nightwatch](https://nightwatch.io/blog/best-peec-ai-alternatives/))

## When We Win vs. Lose

| Scenario | Outcome | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Buyer needs to act on insights, not just see dashboards | **WIN** | Surfaced generates content to close gaps; Peec only reports |
| Buyer wants content generation + quality control in the same tool | **WIN** | Peec has no content capabilities |
| Smaller/newer brand that needs to build AI visibility from scratch | **WIN** | Surfaced's content generation helps build presence; Peec only monitors what already exists |
| Budget-conscious SME comparing entry prices | **WIN** | Surfaced Starter €49/mo with more features vs. Peec Starter €85/mo monitoring only |
| Buyer is primarily an analyst who wants deep monitoring dashboards | **LOSE** | Peec's analytics depth is their core focus — if dashboards are all they want, Peec delivers |
| Buyer values VC backing and company growth trajectory | **LOSE** | Peec's €7M+ funding signals stability that early-stage competitors can't match |

**Deal Killers:** If the prospect's only need is analytics-grade monitoring with daily refresh across many AI models and they have separate content tools, Peec's focused analytics approach may win. Don't fight on the "better dashboard" front — fight on "what you do with the data."

## Trap Questions

1. **"After I see my visibility report, what's the workflow to actually improve my AI visibility inside your platform?"**
   - *Why it matters:* Peec has no content generation or optimisation features. The answer will reveal they're a monitoring tool only.
   - *Good answer:* They walk you through an in-platform workflow from insight to content.
   - *Bad answer:* "You'd export the data and use your existing content tools" — confirming the gap.

2. **"Can I try the platform before committing? What does your free trial look like?"**
   - *Why it matters:* Peec doesn't offer a free trial. This forces a financial commitment sight-unseen.
   - *Good answer:* "Yes, here's a 7-day or 14-day free trial."
   - *Bad answer:* "We don't offer a free trial, but here's a demo" — which isn't the same thing.

3. **"We're a small brand just starting to build AI visibility. How much value will we get from day one?"**
   - *Why it matters:* Peec is best for established brands with existing AI mentions. New brands with minimal mentions will see empty dashboards.
   - *Good answer:* They explain how they help build visibility, not just track it.
   - *Bad answer:* "You need some existing brand presence for the tool to be useful."

4. **"Can we integrate your data with our existing tools via API?"**
   - *Why it matters:* API is gated behind the expensive Enterprise plan — not available on standard tiers.
   - *Good answer:* "API is available on all plans" or at least on mid-tier.
   - *Bad answer:* "API access is on our Enterprise plan" — reveals it's locked behind the highest tier.
